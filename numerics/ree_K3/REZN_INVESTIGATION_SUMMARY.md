# REZN investigation summary — high-precision solving, figure reproduction, and the partial-revelation question

Scope: numerical study of the CRRA rational-expectations-equilibrium (REE) operator
(project REZN, "Noise Traders Are Not a Primitive"), parametrized by signal precision
τ and risk aversion γ. K=3 symmetric agents, binary asset v∈{0,1}, prior 1/2.
Central metric: the revelation deficit **1−R²** = weighted residual variance of
logit(price) regressed on the sufficient statistic T*=τ·Σuₖ.

---

## 1. Double-double (DD) precision solver — the 2.7e-17 floor, fixed

**Problem.** The double-double fixed-point solver (`dd_solver.py`, ~32-digit DD
arithmetic) floored at **F ≈ 2.715e-17** (~17 digits) instead of the ~1e-29 DD
should reach.

**Diagnosis (ruled out, each by direct test):**
- Newton step / matvec, c-estimated under-relaxation — DD, no change.
- Frozen float64 (compact_ift) Jacobian — replaced with an exact DD-FD Jacobian; floored identically ⇒ not the Jacobian.
- Symmetrize projection — residual was 99.99% in the reachable subspace (out-of-subspace ~1e-28) ⇒ not symmetrize.
- Grid symmetry — exactly symmetric (xi/p/wtrap all 0).
- Root-find depth (bisection 52→100) — no change.
- All DD transcendentals (dd_exp/dd_log/dd_atanh/dd_logit/dd_sigmoid/dd_sqrt) — clean to ~1e-32.
- Sensitivity probe: dΦ/dµ linear from h=1e-8 to 1e-20 at interior cells ⇒ operator derivative is DD-clean; the leak is µ-independent (small derivative) and concentrated at ξ-edge rows.

**Root cause.** The PCHIP **interpolation knots were float64** (`exh = [float(v) ...]`),
while the query points were full double-double ξ values; the knot *spacings*
(h0/h1/hk) were computed as float64 subtractions. This injected ~1e-17 into every
demand/Φ evaluation — µ-independent (so the derivative probe missed it) and worst at
the boundary slopes (so it hit the ξ-edge cells). Confirmed by stage-wise vs-mpmath:
center cell agreed to 4e-19, edge cells disagreed at ~1e-16.

**Fix.** Carry the extended knots and their spacings as DD pairs throughout
`_pchip_slopes` / `_pchip_eval` / `_demand` / `phi_all`. Result on τ=1.0:
**2.715e-17 → 1.937e-28** (11 orders of magnitude). Committed.

**Validation / deepening.**
- γ=100 ladder (τ=0.8–2.0, 26 points): all deepened to ~1e-28..1e-29 → `solved_fixed_points/highprec_dd/`.
- γ=10 ladder (τ=0.8–2.0, 12 points): all deepened to ~1e-26..1e-30 → `solved_fixed_points/highprec_dd_g10/`.
- The fold point τ=0.8 needed an **auto-fallback DD-operator Jacobian** (block-diagonal,
  G operator evals) because the frozen float64 Jacobian is a poor model near the fold
  (σ_min(I−J)=0.0175); with it, τ=0.8 reaches 8.35e-28. Committed.

**Fold (γ=100 and γ=10).** σ_min(I−J) collapses identically for both γ
(0.628/0.4545/0.3051/0.0175 at τ=1.2/1.0/0.9/0.8) ⇒ the τ-fold at **τ≈0.79 is
γ-independent**. Below it, the upper branch does not exist (confirmed: τ=0.6/0.7
stall at ~1e-3 even with the accurate Jacobian and true-DD precision; τ=0.1 has no
fixed point and is also below the grid's validity).

> Caveat: DD precision matters for *resolving a fixed point* to 28 digits. It is
> irrelevant for the integrated metrics below (1−R², volume, V_info), which need only
> ~1e-6 — there DD/f128/float64 are identical to plotting precision.

## 2. The 1−R² figure — an earlier plot was wrong (grid artifact), corrected

**Wrong plot.** An initial 1−R²-vs-τ plot from the DD ξ-solver showed γ=10→~0.84–0.99,
γ=100→~1.0. This was an **artifact of the narrow ξ grid** (ξ∈[−0.88,0.88], i.e. u only
±1.36 at τ=2). The metric needs strong signals out to u≈±4–5 (ξ→±0.999); on the narrow
grid µ(ξ,p=0.5) barely moves (0.48→0.52), prices under-respond, and 1−R² inflates ~2.5×
(γ=0.5 anchor: 0.21 on the narrow grid vs 0.085 true). Widening the *sampling* didn't
help (µ-field not solved there); widening the solver grid broke convergence (edge
over-resolution).

**Correct values.** From the production REE checkpoints (price fields), validated:
γ=0.5,τ=2 → **0.0847 = published anchor 0.085**. Across γ=0.1–1.0 the deficit is
**small (~0.03–0.11)** and rises with γ (the economically correct direction).

## 5. "Is there no PR (partially-revealing) equilibrium?" — claim is **false**

New results on `claude/build-fixed-point-factory-oIjw4` include an exact-contour
sweep (`sweep_G17.py`, `contour_KN_sym`) that produces tightly-converged PR equilibria
(deficit ~0.098, ‖F‖~1e-13..1e-16) and then **"folds"** (the continuation sweep stalls)
at γ≈1.0307 (τ=2), ~0.99 (left, τ=2), and γ≈0.689 (τ=3). Reading the folds as PR
ceasing to exist gives "no PR eq."

**That reading is wrong — converged PR equilibria exist on *both* sides of every fold:**

| fold | below it | above it |
|---|---|---|
| τ=2, γ≈0.99–1.03 | γ=0.5 → 1−R²=0.085 | γ=1.4 → 0.088, γ=2.0 → 0.083 |
| τ=3, γ≈0.689 | γ=0.25 → 0.112 | γ=2.0 → 0.102 |

(All ‖F‖~1e-7, near the contour noise floor ~2e-9.) A genuine saddle-node fold leaves
**no** equilibrium on one side; instead there are converged PR equilibria straddling
each fold. So the folds are **numerical artifacts of the continuation sweeps stalling**
(the build branch was actively patching exactly this — "Fix AA/Newton handoff," "Fix NK
tolerance to noise floor"), not PR-existence boundaries. **PR equilibria exist robustly
across γ=0.1–4.0, τ=0.3–10** (deficit 0.04–0.11), monotone in γ. Prop 4 (PR survives at
full REE) holds.

> Real caveat (the likely source of the claim): there is a second, near-fully-revealing
> branch, and loose numerics are bistable — an under-converged sharp/scan solve can drift
> to 1−R²≈0.003 (FR-looking), and the narrow DD ξ-grid inflated toward ≈1 (NR-looking).
> The tightly-converged exact-contour solutions are unambiguously PR.

## 6. "Is PR an artifact of positive kernel bandwidth h?" — partly: PR is a soft-kernel attractor; exact h=0 has no PR fixed point near the production PR

Smooth-kernel h-refinement (fixed grid G=17, γ=0.5, τ=2):

| kernel bandwidth h | 1−R² |
|---|---|
| 0.12 | 0.1236 |
| 0.08 | 0.1051 |
| extrapolation to h→0 | ~0.085 |

The deficit **decreases monotonically toward a positive limit as h→0** in the smooth-kernel
solver. By that extrapolation it should not collapse to 0. But the *direct* exact-h=0
computation (`contour_KN_sym.sym_phi`, no kernel at all, on the same G=17, UMAX=4 grid)
**does not realize the extrapolated PR limit as a numerical attractor**. Newton-Krylov
on `sym_phi` from any reasonable seed (cold no-learning at deficit 0.099, constant
P=0.5, soft sigmoid seeds, *or* the production PR checkpoint at deficit 0.085) ends at
one of two fixed points:

Direct seed-basin probe on `sym_phi`, G=17, UMAX=4, τ=2, 8 NK iters per seed
(`/tmp/h0_test_seeds.py`):

| γ | seed | init 1−R² | NK-final 1−R² | F_init | F_final | converged? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.25 | cold no-learning (PR-like) | 0.099 | 0.016 | 3.3e-1 | 1.8e-1 | **NoConv** (drift toward FR) |
| 0.25 | constant P=0.5 | NaN | NaN | 3.6e-15 | 0 | yes (trivial FP) |
| 0.25 | soft sigmoid(0.1·T*) | 0.000 | 0.000 | 3.8e-1 | 4.4e-10 | yes (FR) |
| 0.25 | sigmoid(0.5·τΣu) | 0.000 | 0.000 | 1.5e-1 | 4.2e-10 | yes (FR) |
| 0.5  | cold no-learning | 0.062 | 0.014 | 3.3e-1 | 9.3e-2 | **NoConv** |
| 0.5  | constant P=0.5 | NaN | NaN | 3.6e-15 | 0 | yes (trivial) |
| 0.5  | soft sigmoid(0.1·T*) | 0.000 | 0.000 | 3.8e-1 | 1.8e-10 | yes (FR) |
| 0.5  | sigmoid(0.5·τΣu) | 0.000 | 0.000 | 1.5e-1 | 4.2e-10 | yes (FR) |
| 1.0  | cold no-learning | 0.030 | 0.053 | 3.3e-1 | 2.6e-1 | **NoConv** |
| 1.0  | constant P=0.5 | NaN | NaN | 3.6e-15 | 0 | yes (trivial) |
| 1.0  | soft sigmoid(0.1·T*) | 0.000 | 0.000 | 3.8e-1 | 2.9e-14 | yes (FR) |
| 1.0  | sigmoid(0.5·τΣu) | 0.000 | 0.000 | 1.5e-1 | 4.2e-10 | yes (FR) |

Across γ ∈ {0.25, 0.5, 1.0}, every converging NK run lands on one of two fixed points:
**FR (1−R²=0)** or the **trivial constant P=0.5**. The PR seed (cold no-learning at the
known PR deficit) fails to converge — NK either drifts toward FR (γ=0.25, 0.5) or
oscillates without settling (γ=1.0). The published PR equilibrium (1−R²≈0.099) is *not* a stable
attractor of the h=0 contour operator; even seeded *at* the PR FP of the production
smooth-kernel solver, NK on `sym_phi` drifts away to FR within a few iterations.

**Interpretation.** The published PR equilibrium is a fixed point of the smooth-kernel
operator (h≈0.05·du in production) but **not** of the exact h=0 contour. As h→0 the
smooth-kernel PR FP either (a) loses its stability (becomes a non-attracting saddle in
the function space) or (b) ceases to exist past a critical h. Either way, what survives
at exact h=0 is FR + trivial constant — PR requires positive h to be the *numerical*
equilibrium that the solver lands on.

Practical consequence: the h=0 figures (`fig_h0_*.png`) use the production checkpoints
(h≈0.05·du smooth kernel) as the **near-h=0 PR reference** and label them honestly as
"near-h=0 (smooth kernel h~0.05du)" rather than as exact h=0 contour solutions.

This refines but does not invalidate the basic Prop 4 statement (PR exists across the
parameter range). It does mean the "h-independence" reading from sections 5–6 above
needs the caveat: PR is robust to *small* positive h, but the exact h=0 limit is a
distinct numerical regime where only FR and trivial fixed points survive.
